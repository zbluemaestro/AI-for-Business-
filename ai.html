⚡ PRACTICAL LAB — Week 1

Your first AI prompt session

~1 hour · tools: Claude.ai or ChatGPT · no signup cost
1

Open Claude.ai or ChatGPT. Write a prompt asking it to explain your current job or business in simpler terms — see how it responds.

2

Ask it to write a short professional bio for you. Edit the output to sound like you. This is the core vibe-coding skill: prompt → read → correct → ship.

3

Ask it three business questions you've genuinely wondered about. Notice where it's confident versus vague — this is how you start calibrating trust.

⚡ PRACTICAL LAB — Week 3

Build your first real AI product — a personal landing page

~2 hrs · tools: bolt.new · zero setup · free
1

Go to bolt.new. Type: "Build me a clean, modern personal landing page. My name is [your name]. I help [what you do]. Dark background, blue accents. Include a short bio, 3 services I offer, and a contact button."

2

Read what it generates. Find one thing wrong or that you want changed. Tell it specifically: "Change the heading font to be larger" or "Remove the pricing section."

3

Iterate 3–5 times until it looks like something you'd actually share. This single session teaches you more about vibe coding than 3 hours of tutorials.

4

Download the HTML file. You now have a real deliverable you built without writing a single line of code.

TOOLbolt.new — Build in browser, no setup needed

Publish your first digital product on Gumroad

~3 hrs · tools: Claude + Canva + Gumroad · goal: live product page
1

Pick a topic you know something about — your profession, a hobby, a city, a workflow. Prompt Claude: "Write a practical 10-page ebook on [topic] for beginners. Use clear headings, bullet points, and include 5 actionable tips per section."

2

Open Canva. Use a free ebook template. Paste Claude's content, choose a cover image, export as PDF. Total design time: 30 minutes.

3

Create a free Gumroad account. Upload the PDF. Set the price at $5–$15 or "pay what you want." Write your product description using Claude.

4

Share the link. You now have a passive income product — it sells whether you're working or sleeping.

⚡ FINAL LAB — Week 14

Offer your AI automation service to one real business — for free

Goal: your first real client and testimonial
1

Identify one local business, restaurant, salon, or small brand that has no AI workflow. Reach out — by email, DM, or in person. Offer to set up one free automation for them using n8n (e.g., auto-reply to WhatsApp enquiries, or weekly social post scheduling).

2

Do the work. It should take you 2–3 hours maximum using what you learned in Weeks 6–7. Use Claude to write the prompts inside the workflow.

3

Ask for a written testimonial or a short video review. This becomes your first real social proof — more valuable than any course certificate.

4

Charge the second client. $150–$500 for a basic automation setup is a normal market rate in 2025–2026. You now have a business.
